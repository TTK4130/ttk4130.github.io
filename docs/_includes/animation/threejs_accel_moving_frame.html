1. Centripetal acceleration
\(P\) is fixed in the rotating frame. The centripetal term \(\boldsymbol{\omega}\times(\boldsymbol{\omega}\times\mathbf{r}_{P/b})\) always points inward toward the rotation axis.
2. Coriolis acceleration
\(P\) slides along the rotating arm. The Coriolis term \(2\boldsymbol{\omega}\times\tfrac{d^b}{dt}\mathbf{r}_{P/b}\) acts perpendicular to the sliding motion.
